1. Main Coffee Species (Commercially Used)

In the world market there are 4 primary species, but 2 dominate almost all coffee production.

Species	% of World Production	Flavor
Arabica	~60–70%	smooth, complex, sweet
Robusta	~30–40%	strong, bitter, higher caffeine
Liberica	<1%	smoky, woody
Excelsa (a Liberica variant)	<1%	tart, fruity

So almost every coffee you drink is Arabica or Robusta.

2. Varieties of Arabica (This Is Where the Big Number Comes From)

Within Arabica, there are 100+ varieties developed over time.

Common ones:

Variety	Flavor
Bourbon	sweet, chocolate
Typica	clean, balanced
Caturra	bright, citrus
Catuai	mild, sweet
Gesha (Geisha)	floral, tea-like
Pacamara	fruity
SL28	wine-like
SL34	berry

Many farms grow specific cultivars adapted to their region.

3. Flavor Differences by Region

Even the same bean variety tastes different depending on where it's grown.

Region	Typical Flavor
Colombia	chocolate, caramel
Brazil	nutty, cocoa
Ethiopia	floral, fruity
Kenya	berry, wine
Guatemala	chocolate, spice
Sumatra	earthy, heavy

This is similar to wine terroir.

4. Roast Levels (Another Major Variable)

The same bean can taste very different depending on roast:

Roast	Flavor
Light	fruity, acidic
Medium	balanced
Medium-dark	chocolate, caramel
Dark	smoky, bold
5. Total Realistic Combinations

If you combine:

4 species

100+ varieties

30+ coffee-growing regions

4 roast levels

You easily get thousands of possible coffee flavors.

✅ Simple takeaway

2 beans dominate: Arabica and Robusta

100+ Arabica varieties

30+ growing regions

4 main roast levels

Which means there are thousands of unique coffee profiles.
