Yes — you can absolutely do with coffee what you’ve been doing with ice cream and cookies: mix components to create a repeatable “recipe.” In coffee, the variables are just a little different. ☕️

Think of coffee drinks like a formula made of these parts:

Bean blend (flavor base)

Roast level (strength/body)

Extraction strength (coffee-to-water ratio)

Milk type and amount

Sweetener type and amount

Flavor additions (vanilla, caramel, etc.)

If you standardize those pieces, you can design your own drinks exactly like recipes.

1. Coffee Bean Blends (Flavor Base)

Coffee flavor starts with the beans and roast level.

Typical Blend Profiles
Blend Type	Bean Origin	Flavor Profile
Breakfast Blend	Central America	light, smooth
House Blend	Mixed regions	balanced
Espresso Blend	Brazil + Colombia	chocolate, nutty
Dark Roast	Indonesia/Africa	bold, smoky

Example custom blend:

“Morning Smooth Blend”

60% Colombian

30% Brazilian

10% Ethiopian

Flavor: chocolate + mild fruit.

2. Brew Strength (Coffee-to-Water Ratio)

Strength is controlled by ratio.

Strength	Ratio
Light	1:18
Standard	1:16
Strong	1:14
Very strong	1:12

Example:

Strong drip coffee

30 g coffee

420 g water

3. Latte Milk Ratios

A latte is basically:

espresso + steamed milk + foam

Typical ratios:

Drink	Espresso	Milk	Foam
Latte	1 part	4–6 parts	thin
Cappuccino	1	3	thick
Flat White	1	3–4	very thin
Cortado	1	1	none

Example latte:

2 shots espresso (36 g)

200 ml milk

microfoam

4. Sugar / Sweetness Levels

Just like cookies or ice cream, sweetness can be standardized.

Sweet Level	Sugar
Unsweet	0
Light	1 tsp
Medium	2 tsp
Sweet	1 tbsp
Dessert	2 tbsp

Or syrup:

Flavor syrup	Amount
Light	½ oz
Standard	1 oz
Sweet	1.5 oz
5. Flavor Profiles (Coffee “Recipes”)

You can design drinks like this:

Vanilla Latte

Honey Latte
